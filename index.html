ⓘ Method, sources & assumptions

Method — three layers, kept separate

Measured figures come directly from a published dataset and are cited. Modelled figures are derived by inference or spatial join; each carries its assumption chain and a confidence label. Judgement inputs are planner assertions, marked as such — none are applied in Ship 1. No blended score exists: a route gets target boardings (volume) and an audience index (concentration), never one number.

Sources

    Stated assumptions (Ship 1)

      Confidence labels

      Subzone-based segments start at medium; planning-area segments start at low (coarser geometry). The label widens when less than 80% of a route's allocated volume has audience data, or when more than 10% comes from index-capped subzones. Nothing is labelled better than medium: the residence join (boarders ≈ catchment residents) is unverified and applies to every figure.

      Ethnicity

      Ethnic composition is shown per route and subzone as creative and language guidance only — festive timing, language mix. It is not a selectable audience filter and enters no score, by design.

      Resonance

      Not scored — it cannot be derived from open data. It is measured post-campaign (brand-lift vs matched control), never asserted pre-campaign.
